## 1. Brand positioning

### 1.1 What PettahPro is

A cloud accounting and business operations platform built specifically for how Sri Lankan SMEs actually operate — with Pettah wholesalers, Kandy retailers, and Colombo service businesses as the primary audience.

### 1.2 Positioning statement

For Sri Lankan SMEs who've outgrown paper books or feel trapped in desktop BUSY/Tally installations, PettahPro is the cloud accounting platform that understands SL business reality — VAT, WHT, EPF, PAYE, cheques, multiple currencies, wearing-many-hats operators — without forcing them to learn accounting software designed for New York or Mumbai.

### 1.3 What PettahPro is not

- **Not a Silicon Valley tool made to fit SL** — too many SaaS products assume a US/UK business context that breaks when applied to Pettah
- **Not enterprise software pretending to be SME software** — SAP and Oracle down-market products carry too much complexity for a 15-person business
- **Not a glorified calculator** — we're not competing with Excel
- **Not a Tally replacement that's "also on the cloud"** — we rethink the experience for the cloud era, not port old patterns forward
- **Not cheap** — Growth tier matches BUSY year-1 cost; positioned on value over 3 years, not price

### 1.4 Target audience

**Primary: SME owners and their teams**
- Business size: 5-100 employees
- Revenue: LKR 10M-500M/year
- Location: Colombo (especially Pettah), Kandy, Galle, Negombo, Kurunegala
- Industries: wholesale/distribution, retail, pharmacy, restaurants, small manufacturing, services
- Tech comfort: moderate — comfortable with WhatsApp and online banking, not necessarily with traditional accounting software
- Pain points: BUSY/Tally desktop license headaches, no remote access, manual cheque tracking, VAT/WHT compliance anxiety, staff turnover eroding institutional knowledge

**Secondary: Accountants and CAs**
- Serve multiple SME clients
- Often the de-facto advisor for which accounting system clients adopt
- Need audit trails, standard SL-compliant reports, period-close rigor
- Hate having to re-learn each client's system

**Tertiary: Cashiers, stock keepers, sales staff**
- Daily operational users
- Often less tech-savvy
- Need fast, simple screens that don't require accounting knowledge

### 1.5 Competitive positioning

| Competitor | How PettahPro beats them |
|---|---|
| **BUSY / Tally** | Cloud-native, mobile-ready, no license transfer hassle, migration included, AI-assisted entry |
| **Zoho Books** | SL-native (VAT, WHT, EPF, PAYE, cheque lifecycle per SL Bounced Cheques Act), local bank file formats, Tamil/Sinhala support |
| **QuickBooks** | SL compliance built-in (QB needs endless customization), Pettah-context features (cheques, walk-in cash sales, on-account credit) |
| **SAP Business One** | 1/100th the cost, deployed in days not months, no consultants required |
| **Excel / Paper books** | Audit-trail enforced, multi-user, statutory filings auto-prepared, no data loss |

### 1.6 Strategic differentiators

1. **Sri Lanka-native** — VAT/WHT/EPF/PAYE/SSCL baked in, not bolted on
2. **Migration-first** — 30-day parallel run with UiPath bot from day one; this is the wedge
3. **Minimal-entry UX** — photo/scan/barcode-first, manual entry as fallback
4. **Wearing-many-hats aware** — multi-role permission model for real SL SME team structures
5. **Compliance confidence** — regulatory updates auto-roll to tenants; no "did I miss a VAT rate change?" anxiety
6. **Cloud that works like local** — offline POS, low-bandwidth mode, WhatsApp-ready (Phase 2)

## 2. Brand personality and voice

### 2.1 Brand personality

If PettahPro were a person:

- **Competent** — knows accounting deeply, knows SL compliance deeply
- **Practical** — solves the real problem, doesn't moralize about how you should run your business
- **Direct** — says what needs to be said without corporate fluff
- **Warm** — understands you're running a real business with real pressures, not an abstract case study
- **Modern but not trendy** — uses technology to solve problems, not to chase buzzwords
- **Locally rooted** — speaks the language (literally and figuratively), knows what "hisab kitaab" means

**Not**:
- Stiff or bureaucratic
- Silicon Valley hyped ("revolutionize!", "disrupt!", "10x!")
- Cute or cartoonish
- Condescending ("let us teach you accounting")
- Fear-mongering ("avoid IRD penalties!")

### 2.2 Voice attributes

| Attribute | We are | We are not |
|---|---|---|
| Clarity | "VAT filing due 20 Apr" | "Don't forget your important compliance obligations!" |
| Directness | "Your trial ends in 3 days" | "Just a friendly reminder that you may want to consider..." |
| Warmth | "Nice work — first invoice sent" | "Great job champion!" |
| Expertise | "WHT on professional services: 5%" | "You might need to deduct some tax" |
| Humility | "We got this wrong — here's the fix" | "Our world-class system..." |
| Practicality | "Skip setup for now — come back anytime" | "You must complete onboarding to proceed" |

### 2.3 Writing principles

1. **Say what it is** — "Invoice posted" beats "Transaction successfully recorded to ledger"
2. **Respect the reader's time** — one scan, one understanding. No buried leads.
3. **Lead with the verb** — "Send invoice" beats "Click here to send"
4. **Numbers are facts, not features** — "LKR 5,234" beats "A whopping LKR 5,234"
5. **Explain only when asked** — inline help via "i" icons, not blocks of explanatory prose
6. **No "just"** — "Just click here" sounds like an apology. Click here.
7. **No emoji in product UI** — we're accounting software, not a consumer app. (Marketing may use them sparingly.)

### 2.4 Voice by context

| Context | Example | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Transactional success** | "Invoice INV-2026-0342 sent to Fathima Importers" | Confirms what happened, no fluff |
| **Error** | "Couldn't send invoice — email address invalid" | States the problem directly, implies the fix |
| **Empty state** | "No invoices yet. Create your first one to see it here." | Friendly, actionable |
| **Destructive confirmation** | "This will permanently delete INV-2026-0342. Type the invoice number to confirm." | Serious tone for serious action |
| **Helpful tip** | "Tip: Customers over credit limit need Owner override" | Inline expertise, not lecturing |
| **Marketing** | "Built for how Pettah actually does business" | Confident, specific, on-brand |
| **Error for end-customer (invoice recipient)** | "This invoice is no longer available" | Generic, protects tenant's privacy |

### 2.5 Inclusive language

- Use gender-neutral language ("their" not "his/her")
- Avoid idioms that don't translate (no "hit a home run", no "jumping the shark")
- Prefer universally understood words ("paid in full" over "settled", though "settled" is also fine in accounting context)
- Cultural sensitivity: avoid religious or politically charged imagery in product UI
- Use "business owner" or "your team" rather than "entrepreneur" (feels too aspirational for Pettah context)

## 3. Brand name

### 3.1 Why "PettahPro"

**Pettah** — The wholesale heart of Sri Lanka. A claim on the market's real center of gravity. Universally recognized across SL business community.

**Pro** — Professional-grade software. Serious tool for serious businesses.

**Together**: Clear positioning — professional tools for the market that drives SL commerce.

### 3.2 Name usage rules

- Always **one word**, CamelCase: **PettahPro**
- Never PETTAHPRO (all caps), Pettahpro (all lowercase), Pettah Pro (two words with space), or Pettah-Pro (hyphenated)
- In body copy, first mention: **PettahPro**. Subsequent mentions in same paragraph: also PettahPro (never shorten to "Pettah" or "the platform")
- Possessive: PettahPro's (e.g., "PettahPro's pricing")
- Never use "PP" as abbreviation — reads as childish
- Never translate the name

### 3.3 Domain and digital presence

- Primary domain: **pettahpro.lk** (to be registered)
- Alternates to register (defensive): pettahpro.com, getpettahpro.com, mypettahpro.com
- Social handles: **@pettahpro** (Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook)
- App stores: **PettahPro** (no suffix, no "LK")
- Email domain: **@pettahpro.lk** for team, **@get.pettahpro.lk** or **@noreply.pettahpro.lk** for transactional

### 3.4 Legal

- Trademark search to be completed (SL + WIPO)
- Domain squatting check: ensure no squatters on key variants
- Social handle availability check on major platforms

## 4. Logo system

### 4.1 Wordmark

The primary logo is a wordmark (text-only logo).

**Specification**:
- Typography: **Inter 500 weight** (medium), tracking -0.01em
- Word: `PettahPro` (two words visually connected as one, CamelCase)
- No underline, no italic, no special effects
- Primary color: `#1A1A1A` (charcoal) on light backgrounds
- Reverse color: `#FAFAF9` (off-white) on dark/mint backgrounds

**ASCII representation of intended look**:
```
PettahPro
```

**Proportions**:
- Cap height: 32px (at base size 48px logo height)
- Letterforms follow Inter 500 exact proportions
- No custom letter modifications

### 4.2 Mark / icon

A standalone mark for favicons, app icons, social avatars, and small spaces where full wordmark doesn't fit.

**Specification**:
- Square container with rounded corners (8px radius at 64px icon size)
- Background: solid charcoal `#1A1A1A`
- Foreground: single letter `P` in Inter 500, color `#7FB89A` (mint)
- Letter positioned slightly optical-center adjusted (not geometrically centered)
- Variant: mint background `#7FB89A` with charcoal `P` for lighter surfaces

**Use cases**:
- Browser favicon (16×16, 32×32)
- iOS / Android app icon (1024×1024 master)
- Social media avatar (400×400)
- In-product user menu header (24×24)

### 4.3 Clear space

Minimum clear space around the logo equals the height of the letter "P" in the wordmark.

```
  [  ] P e t t a h P r o [  ]
  [P]                    [P]
  [  ]                   [  ]
```

No other logo, text, or graphic element should enter this clear space.

### 4.4 Minimum sizes

- **Wordmark**: 80px wide minimum on screen. Never below 60px — letterforms break down.
- **Mark**: 16px minimum (favicon). 24px minimum for most UI use.

### 4.5 Color variants

| Variant | Background | Logo color | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | White / off-white | Charcoal `#1A1A1A` | Default — website, product, documents |
| Reverse | Charcoal `#1A1A1A` | Off-white `#FAFAF9` | Dark backgrounds, footers, video |
| Mint | Mint `#7FB89A` (dark mint surfaces) | Charcoal `#1A1A1A` | Mint-themed marketing materials |
| Mint reverse | Charcoal `#1A1A1A` | Mint `#7FB89A` | Dark hero sections |
| Single-color mint | White | Mint `#7FB89A` | Casual/secondary placements |

### 4.6 Logo don'ts

Do not:

- Change the typeface to anything other than Inter 500
- Stretch, squash, or distort
- Add a drop shadow, glow, gradient, or 3D effect
- Place over busy photographic backgrounds without a solid overlay
- Rotate (not even slightly)
- Add visual effects (outline, emboss, bevel)
- Add tagline next to the wordmark (tagline is separate — see section 9)
- Use the wordmark smaller than 60px wide
- Recolor outside the approved palette
- Use as a word in running text ("we use PettahPro" — here PettahPro is just the word, styled as body text, not a logo)

## 5. Color system

### 5.1 Palette overview — Charcoal & Mint

A deliberately restrained palette. Primary is near-black for trust and professionalism. Mint is the singular accent — used for positive actions, confirmations, progress, and brand moments. Everything else is neutral.

This palette ages well, photographs well, prints well (both color and grayscale), and reads differently from the generic blue that dominates SL fintech.

### 5.2 Primary colors

| Token | Hex | RGB | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--color-charcoal` | `#1A1A1A` | 26, 26, 26 | Primary text, logo, primary buttons, borders on emphasis |
| `--color-mint` | `#7FB89A` | 127, 184, 154 | Accent — CTAs, success states, active nav, brand moments |
| `--color-mint-dark` | `#3D6B52` | 61, 107, 82 | Mint hover state, text on mint backgrounds |
| `--color-off-white` | `#FAFAF9` | 250, 250, 249 | Primary page background |
| `--color-mint-surface` | `#E8EDE9` | 232, 237, 233 | Secondary surface / sidebar background |

### 5.3 Neutrals

| Token | Hex | RGB | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--color-black` | `#1A1A1A` | 26, 26, 26 | Same as charcoal — primary text and strong contrast |
| `--color-text-primary` | `#1A1A1A` | 26, 26, 26 | Headings, body text, primary interactive labels |

Used sparingly — only when state communication is necessary.

| Token | Hex | Use |

| `--color-success-bg` | `#E8EDE9` | Success banner backgrounds |
| `--color-warning` | `#B47A15` (saffron-dark) | "Overdue soon", "Needs attention", "Over limit" |

| Token | Hex | Use in charts |
|---|---|---|
| `--chart-1` | `#3D6B52` | Primary series (mint-dark) |

### 5.6 Contrast verification

All text/background combinations must meet WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).

| Text | On background | Contrast ratio | Pass |
|---|---|---|---|
| `#1A1A1A` | `#FAFAF9` | 17.7:1 | AAA |
| `#1A1A1A` | `#E8EDE9` | 14.5:1 | AAA |
| `#1A1A1A` | `#7FB89A` | 10.1:1 | AAA |
| `#FAFAF9` | `#1A1A1A` | 17.7:1 | AAA |
| `#5F5E5A` | `#FAFAF9` | 6.8:1 | AA |
| `#888780` | `#FAFAF9` | 4.5:1 | AA (borderline — avoid for body text) |
| `#3D6B52` | `#E8EDE9` | 5.1:1 | AA |

**Fail**: Do not use `#888780` text on backgrounds darker than `#F1EFE8`. Reserve for timestamps and large-font hints only.

### 5.7 Usage guidelines

**80/15/5 rule**:
- 80% of any screen should be neutrals (off-white, mint-surface, charcoal text)
- 15% should be structural color (borders, subtle backgrounds)
- 5% should be mint or semantic accents

A dashboard drowning in mint pills and green charts is off-brand. Mint should feel like finding a good piece of news — rare enough to matter.

**When to use mint**:
- Primary CTA buttons (the main action on screen)
- Active navigation item highlight
- Success confirmations ("Paid", "Reconciled")
- Brand moments (landing page hero, first-invoice-sent celebration)
- Progress completion indicators

**When not to use mint**:
- Generic "OK" buttons in confirmation dialogs — charcoal
- Hyperlinks in body text — charcoal with underline
- Form field focus — thin charcoal border, not mint
- Data visualizations unless showing "current" or "primary" series

## 6. Typography

### 6.1 Typeface

**Primary: Inter** (Google Fonts, open source)

Chosen because:
- Designed for computer screens — optimal legibility at small sizes
- Wide weight range (100-900) and 4 width variants
- Excellent Latin character support
- Good-but-not-native Tamil and Sinhala support (will pair with local fallbacks)
- Used by Stripe, GitHub, Figma — reads as modern and trusted
- Free and well-maintained

**Tamil fallback**: Noto Sans Tamil
**Sinhala fallback**: Noto Sans Sinhala

Font stack:
```css
font-family: 'Inter', 'Noto Sans Sinhala', 'Noto Sans Tamil', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif;
```

### 6.2 Weights

Only two weights in use:

| Weight | CSS value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Regular | 400 | Body text, labels, most UI |
| Medium | 500 | Headings, buttons, emphasis, active nav |

**Never use**: 300 (too thin on SL SME's older monitors), 600 (too heavy, reads aggressive), 700+ (marketing only, never in product UI)

### 6.3 Type scale

Desktop scale. Mobile reduces by 1px-2px where noted in Part 9 of UX docs.

| Level | Size | Line height | Weight | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display | 44px | 1.15 | 500 | Landing page hero only |
| H1 | 32px | 1.25 | 500 | Page titles |
| H2 | 22px | 1.35 | 500 | Section headers |
| H3 | 18px | 1.4 | 500 | Card headers, sub-sections |
| H4 | 16px | 1.5 | 500 | Minor sections |
| Body large | 16px | 1.6 | 400 | Primary body text |
| Body | 14px | 1.5 | 400 | Default UI text |
| Small | 13px | 1.45 | 400 | Secondary info, table cells |
| Caption | 12px | 1.4 | 400 | Timestamps, hints |
| Micro | 11px | 1.3 | 500 | Labels (all-uppercase), badges |

### 6.4 Typography rules

1. **Sentence case everywhere**. No Title Case. No ALL CAPS (except Micro labels where appropriate).
2. **No italic**. Except for very rare emphasis in body copy or legal disclaimers.
3. **Max line length: 75 characters** for body text. Narrower for sidebar text.
4. **Numeric tabular display** uses `font-feature-settings: 'tnum'` to align columns of numbers.
5. **Currency with number**: `LKR 5,234.00` — space between LKR and number, two decimals always for currency.
6. **Dates**: `19/04/2026` — DD/MM/YYYY, always padded with zeros.
7. **Time**: `14:35` (24-hour) or `2:35 PM` (12-hour) — tenant-configurable, default 24-hour for SL.
8. **Ellipsis for truncation**: use CSS `text-overflow: ellipsis`, not a literal "..." in text.
9. **No all-caps buttons**. "Save" beats "SAVE".
10. **Paragraph spacing**: 1em below paragraphs, never indent first line.

### 6.5 Text styles by context

```css
/* Primary text */
.text-primary { color: #1A1A1A; font-weight: 400; }

/* Secondary info */
.text-secondary { color: #5F5E5A; font-weight: 400; }

/* Tertiary / placeholders */
.text-tertiary { color: #888780; font-weight: 400; }

/* Numeric (tabular) */
.text-numeric { font-feature-settings: 'tnum'; font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums; }

/* Money */
.text-money { font-feature-settings: 'tnum'; font-weight: 500; }

/* Monospace (for codes, account numbers) */
.text-mono { font-family: 'JetBrains Mono', 'Menlo', monospace; }
```

### 6.6 Multilingual typography

For Tamil and Sinhala content, line heights need extra room due to taller ascenders/descenders.

| Language | Line-height multiplier |
|---|---|
| English | 1.0× (baseline per scale above) |
| Tamil | 1.15× |
| Sinhala | 1.15× |

Font sizes remain identical across languages — only line-height adjusts.

**Mixed language UIs**: If a single label or sentence mixes languages (e.g., "Perera Textiles පුද්ගලික සමාගම"), use the larger line-height of the two languages present.

- No cartoon-style exaggeration
- SL context where relevant (Pettah street scenes, a Colombo skyline hint, tuk-tuk silhouette for logistics empty state)

## 8. Imagery and illustration

### 8.1 Photography style

For marketing contexts (landing page, blog, social media, case studies).

**Style principles**:
- Natural light preferred
- Real environments, not sterile studio shots
- Real SL businesses where possible (with permission) — a Pettah textile wholesaler's shop, a Kandy pharmacy, a Colombo restaurant back office
- Real SL people — no stock photos of Western businesspeople in suits
- Authentic moments — someone checking a phone, reviewing a cheque, at a register
- Mix of wide shots (business context) and tight shots (hands, product details)
- Warm tones in post-processing, not cold corporate blue

**Avoid**:
- Stock photography of generic "diverse team pointing at laptop"
- Shiny Silicon Valley-style "young entrepreneur in hoodie"
- Models posing as accountants
- Overly staged or posed shots
- Heavily filtered Instagram-style processing

### 8.2 Illustration style

For product empty states, onboarding flows, feature callouts, and landing page support.

**Style**: Same visual language as icons — line-based, rounded corners, charcoal + mint.

**Example empty state illustrations** to commission:
1. Empty invoice list — a simple desk/ledger drawing with "+" floating above
2. First-time dashboard — Pettah street vignette with small hand-drawn receipts
3. No customers yet — a shop counter with a bell
4. Migration in progress — boxes being moved from old filing cabinet to cloud icon
5. All synced — a cloud with a checkmark
6. Error state — a simple person holding a broken teacup (SL visual — "teacup" is what accountants call a minor mess)

### 8.3 Photography shot list (for pre-launch photography)

Priority shots to capture in a single photography session:

1. **Pettah context** (exterior) — street scenes, shop fronts, wholesale district
2. **Owner at work** — real business owner reviewing invoices/cheques (with signed releases)
3. **Mobile in-use** — phone on a busy counter showing PettahPro invoice creation
4. **POS in action** — cashier with customer, showing the POS screen naturally
5. **Cheque moment** — someone writing or receiving a cheque
6. **Multi-generational** — older business owner using PettahPro alongside younger team member
7. **Team** — 3-5 person team around a laptop in real office (not rented studio)
8. **Detail shots** — hands, keyboard, a coffee cup next to a laptop showing PettahPro

**Don't have time for photography?** Then use custom illustrations exclusively until photography can be done properly. Bad stock photography is worse than good illustration.

### 8.4 UI screenshot guidelines (for marketing)

When showing PettahPro's product UI in marketing:

- Use realistic SL data — "Perera Textiles", "Kumaran Trading", "LKR 245,000"
- Never use "Acme Inc.", "John Doe", "$100.00"
- Screenshots should be at actual pixel ratio — no scaling artifacts
- Light theme only (dark theme is user-toggleable but brand materials are light)
- Include browser chrome (address bar showing pettahpro.lk) only when context requires — otherwise clean screenshot
- Redact any sensitive data before publishing (but since we use fake data, this shouldn't come up)

## 10. Brand do's and don'ts

### 10.1 Voice and copy

**Do**:
- Write like you're talking to an accountant friend
- Use specific numbers and facts
- Respect the reader's intelligence
- Say what's happening ("Invoice sent") not what was attempted ("We're trying to send your invoice")
- Use SL context naturally (LKR, Pettah, Kandy, real business types)

**Don't**:
- Use buzzwords (revolutionary, game-changer, disrupt, innovative, cutting-edge)
- Use exclamation marks in product UI (rare use in marketing only)
- Apologize excessively ("Sorry for the inconvenience!" — just fix it)
- Use fear/FOMO tactics ("Don't miss out!")
- Use aspirational language disconnected from reality ("Unleash your business potential")
- Capitalize for emphasis (CAPITALIZATION IS SHOUTING)
- Use ellipses... for mystery...
- Call users "guys", "team", "fam", "besties", etc.

### 10.2 Visual

**Do**:
- Use generous whitespace
- Use our palette strictly (charcoal/mint/neutrals + restrained semantic colors)
- Use Inter typeface exclusively
- Use Lucide icons exclusively
- Feature realistic SL data and context in all visuals

**Don't**:
- Use gradients, drop shadows, glows, blur effects, or noise textures
- Mix icon sets
- Use stock photography of non-SL people as "our customers"
- Put logos on busy photographic backgrounds
- Use tables of contents on marketing pages (we're a product, not a legal doc)
- Use comic sans or cursive fonts (anywhere, for any reason)
- Use 3D effects or skeuomorphic design

### 10.3 Color usage

**Do**:
- Use charcoal for 80% of text and chrome
- Reserve mint for primary actions and success states
- Use white space instead of color to create hierarchy

**Don't**:
- Make everything mint-colored — it loses meaning
- Use rainbow palettes (5+ colors in one screen)
- Use pure black (#000000) — always our charcoal (#1A1A1A)
- Use pure white (#FFFFFF) for page backgrounds — use off-white (#FAFAF9)
- Use colors outside the palette for "fun" or "variety"

### 10.4 Imagery

**Do**:
- Show real businesses, real people, real products
- Use SL-specific visual language
- Keep illustrations line-based and consistent with our icons

**Don't**:
- Use stock photos of Western businesspeople
- Use generic "team smiling at laptop" tropes
- Mix illustration styles
- Use 3D renders or isometric illustrations (off-brand)

### 10.5 Layout and spacing

**Do**:
- Use 8px base spacing scale consistently
- Let content breathe — more whitespace beats cramming
- Align elements to a grid

**Don't**:
- Use arbitrary spacing (7px, 13px, 19px — all wrong)
- Make UIs dense like old-school accounting software
- Stretch content to fill space (empty space is fine)

## 12. Brand evolution and maintenance

### 12.1 Brand governance

- **Brand owner**: Founder (Dushy) initially. Designate a brand manager when team > 10.
- **Approval path for brand applications**: Any external-facing asset with PettahPro branding goes through brand owner review until a formal brand manager exists.
- **Version control**: Maintain `brand-kit.md` (this doc) as single source of truth. Versioned in Git.
- **External partners**: Any agency or freelancer producing PettahPro content receives this doc before starting.

### 12.2 Evolution principles

The brand should evolve deliberately, not accidentally.

**When to evolve**:
- Year 2-3 revisit: once we have real customer stories and visual assets, photography becomes central (replacing placeholder illustrations)
- Expansion trigger: if PettahPro expands beyond SL (Bangladesh, Pakistan, India), reconsider the "Pettah" naming and positioning
- Major product redesign: if the product UI evolves significantly, the brand may need complementary refresh

**When not to evolve**:
- Just because we're bored with it
- Because a designer pitches a "fresh look"
- Because a competitor does something different
- Because we hired someone who prefers serif fonts

### 12.4 Common questions

**Q: Can we use a different color for a specific campaign?**
A: Very rarely — requires founder approval. Campaign colors must still sit within the "Extended palette" (section 5.5) and never replace mint/charcoal in our core identity.

**Q: Can we use different fonts for specific contexts?**
A: No. Inter is our voice in visual form. Exception: emails where default system fonts take over (this is expected).

**Q: Can we add emoji to marketing content?**
A: Sparingly — for social media posts where tone warrants it. Never in product UI, never in transactional emails, never in logo or wordmark.

**Q: Can partners/resellers create their own PettahPro-branded materials?**
A: They can use approved templates (see /templates) but cannot create new branded assets without review. We provide a partner brand pack separately.

**Q: What if a customer asks to white-label PettahPro?**
A: White-labeling is an Enterprise-tier consideration. See the pricing architecture spec. Brand team is consulted on any white-label arrangement.

### 12.5 Brand health checks

Quarterly review (founder + design lead):

- Are our marketing materials consistent with this brand kit?
- Is our product UI matching the brand system?
- Are there unauthorized variants creeping in?
- Do customer-facing emails/notifications feel on-brand?
- How are we perceived? (Social mentions, user research, NPS comments)
